# Lenovo Retail Sales Framework

Use this reference when turning Lenovo product information into in-store Chinese sales copy.

## Customer Matching

- Student: emphasize reliability, portability, eye comfort, learning software, battery life, storage, and value. Avoid over-selling professional features unless the student studies design, engineering, media, or gaming.
- Office worker: emphasize smooth multitasking, video meetings, keyboard/trackpad comfort, light weight, ports, battery, and after-sales convenience.
- Business buyer: emphasize stability, security, manageability, build quality, service options, privacy, and long-term total cost. For ThinkPad, keyboard feel and business reliability are usually strong anchors.
- Gamer: emphasize GPU/CPU class, cooling, refresh rate, keyboard, ports, upgrade room, and performance stability. Translate frame-rate or thermal claims only from verified data.
- Creator/designer: emphasize screen quality, color, CPU/GPU workload fit, memory, storage, ports, pen/touch support, and software workflow.
- Family/home: emphasize ease of use, screen size, video calls, storage for photos/classes, child study, space saving, and service.
- Mobile professional: emphasize weight, battery, charging, screen, durability, keyboard, connectivity, and conference experience.

## Category Angles

- ThinkPad: business reliability, keyboard comfort, privacy/security, practical ports, restrained design, service fit.
- Yoga: premium design, portability, screen/audio experience, flexible form factor, creative and mobile office scenarios.
- Legion / Lenovo gaming: performance release, cooling, high refresh display, keyboard, upgradeability, game plus creator workloads.
- Xiaoxin / IdeaPad: value, everyday smoothness, light office/study, screen comfort, approachable price-to-performance.
- Desktop / workstation: stable performance, expandability, long use cycles, easier maintenance, business or creator workloads.
- All-in-one: clean desktop, large screen, family or front-desk use, fewer cables, easy setup.
- Tablet: portability, note-taking, entertainment, kids learning, battery life, pen/keyboard ecosystem when applicable.
- Monitor: screen size, resolution, refresh rate, eye comfort, ergonomics, multi-device productivity.
- Accessories: complete the experience; connect the benefit to a concrete use case such as meetings, commuting, gaming, backup, or desk setup.

## Selling Patterns

- Opening: ask one concrete question before pitching. Example pattern: "您主要是办公出差多，还是在店里想看性能和屏幕体验？"
- Need match: link a verified spec to the customer's use case. Pattern: "这项配置对您来说不是参数堆叠，主要是让多开文档、会议和网页时更稳。"
- Experience guide: invite the customer to touch, type, lift, compare screen, hear fans, check ports, or try common software.
- Proof point: mention official specs, visible hardware, service policy provided by the store, or credible test data. Do not imply internal data if not provided.
- Close: make the next step low-friction: compare two models, confirm color/configuration, check today's store policy, or test a scenario.

## Fact Verification Boundaries

- Confirm the exact model and configuration before writing product-specific highlights or talk tracks.
- Do not infer CPU/GPU, memory, storage, screen, weight, battery, ports, price, stock, warranty, gifts, financing, or promotions from only a brand, series, category, shell design, or similar-looking product.
- If a link or product page shows multiple selectable configurations, ask which configuration the customer is considering before using configuration-specific claims.
- Prefer Lenovo official pages, PSREF, Lenovo support pages, or credible retailer/product pages for current product facts.
- If verification is unavailable, use only user-provided facts and state "未能核验，仅基于用户输入" in `核验/注意`.

## Competitor Comparison Rules

- Compare only with a named product, category, or price band that the customer actually cares about.
- Compare on customer-relevant dimensions: portability, performance stability, screen, keyboard, ports, service, ecosystem, price-to-performance, or business management.
- Use balanced language: "更适合", "优势在", "如果您更看重...", "这台的取向是...".
- Do not attack competitors, claim universal superiority, or cite unverified benchmark/ranking data.
- If competitor facts are not verified, frame the comparison as conditional and place the uncertainty in `核验/注意`.

## Objection Handling

- "价格有点高": return to total value: configuration fit, service, durability, upgrade room, and time saved. Offer to compare a lower/higher configuration instead of pushing.
- "我怕性能不够": ask for actual software/game/tasks, then connect CPU/GPU/memory/cooling to those tasks. Avoid promising all future workloads.
- "会不会太重": invite the customer to lift it with charger if available; explain tradeoff between performance, screen size, ports, and portability.
- "其他品牌也差不多": focus on the customer's deciding factor, then compare one or two dimensions fairly.
- "网上是不是更便宜": avoid price claims unless the store provides policy. Emphasize in-store setup, service, immediate experience, and official channel reassurance where true.

## Cautious Or Prohibited Claims

- Do not claim official warranty, return policy, gift, financing, delivery time, inventory, or price unless the user provides it or a current source confirms it.
- Do not claim health effects such as eye protection beyond certified screen features that are verified.
- Do not claim "AI PC", NPU, Copilot, battery life, military-grade durability, color accuracy, or benchmark performance unless the model/version supports it.
- Do not imply all configurations in one series share the same screen, processor, GPU, memory, weight, battery, or port set.
- Do not use fear-based, discriminatory, or insulting language toward users or competitors.

## Default Drafting Checklist

- Confirm the specific model/configuration and fact source.
- Identify the most likely customer job.
- Select 2-4 benefits that are confirmed or clearly marked as assumptions.
- Write one 80-120 character highlight.
- Write 3-5 talk tracks covering opening, benefit, experience, comparison/closing, and objection handling.
- Add brief evidence or uncertainty in `核验/注意`.
